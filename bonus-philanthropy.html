Bonus Course

Philanthropy

Understanding that giving is a discipline, not just an instinct

01Summary

The philanthropy track replaced a simple, feel-good idea of charity with a much more accurate understanding of how giving actually works as a structured, professional field.

It built in a deliberate progression: starting with vocabulary (time, treasure, and talent), then the difference between a nonprofit that runs its own programs and a foundation that funds other organizations through grants.

02Sessions & Topics Covered

  • Foundation mechanicsCovered how foundations are legally required to give away a minimum percentage of their assets every year, and how grant applications are actually reviewed and scored.
  • Case studyStudied Andrew Carnegie as a historical case in large-scale philanthropy, and how corporate giving fits into a broader Corporate Social Responsibility framework.
  • Founder Q&AClosed with a direct conversation with two real foundation founders, who spoke candidly about how difficult it actually is to choose between multiple deserving applicants.

03Reflection

Giving well requires the same rigor as running a business: budgeting, compliance, measuring impact, and making hard tradeoffs between competing priorities. By the time we heard from the foundation founders, we already had the vocabulary to understand why they described those decisions as genuinely difficult.

04Skills

  • Nonprofit vs. Foundation
  • Grant Review Process
  • Corporate Social Responsibility
  • Impact Measurement
