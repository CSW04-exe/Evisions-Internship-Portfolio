Reflection
Experience, Challenges, and Highlights

Getting to see how a company actually runs day to day was one of the most rewarding parts of this internship, especially watching a fully remote team stay connected and keep growing
Working remotely came with its own learning curve, communication takes more intention when you cannot just walk over and ask
Some departments were genuinely hard to wrap my head around, especially engineering and MAPS, the server that runs Argos, IntelleCheck, and FormFusion behind the scenes

MAPS handles licensing, security, and every database connection for the whole product suite, and seeing how much invisible infrastructure sits behind something as simple as opening an app in a browser was eye opening
Data Engineering had a similar kind of complexity, pulling data from dozens of disconnected campus systems and reshaping it into something usable, and Finance dealt with it too, coordinating budget input across departments where one missing detail can throw off the whole picture
Even Support, which seemed simple at first, required real judgment, triaging issues almost like an emergency room


The clear highlight was the Argos workshop week with Professional Services, learning SQL and finally understanding how the product I had heard about all internship actually works under the hood
Every department left me with something, and looking back through my notes, those small takeaways added up more than I expected

Insights and Advice Gained from Employees

The biggest lesson was how much building real connections matters, not just for opportunity but for actually enjoying the work
Evisions has a strong sense of community, and being around people willing to share their time made me more confident and motivated throughout
Employees also passed along solid career advice, tailor your resume to the actual job instead of using one generic version, quantify your achievements with real numbers, and walk into interviews with a few strong stories ready instead of trying to prepare a separate answer for everything
A small thing that stuck with me, doing a little research on who is interviewing you and finding real common ground turns an interview into a conversation, which people remember

Next Steps After the Internship

I am continuing to apply to jobs and staying open to where things lead
I plan to keep building on the connections made here, they feel worth holding onto beyond the internship itself
I want to keep developing the skills I picked up, especially around data and reporting, and how a product actually moves from idea to launch

Advice for Anyone in a Similar Position

Take remote communication seriously, over communicate and ask questions early instead of waiting for context to come to you
Do not shy away from the departments that feel too technical at first, sitting with the discomfort is usually how it starts to click
Treat every conversation as a chance to build a real connection, not just information to write down
Start applying the career advice early, tailored resumes, real stories, interviews as conversations
Stay curious and let yourself enjoy it, the people are just as valuable as the technical skills
