Bonus Course

Connections

Building the habit of genuine relationship building early in a career

01Summary

Connections was not taught as one standalone track. It was woven through nearly everything. The goal was to instill one habit: treating every professional relationship as worth investing in intentionally, starting from the very beginning of a career rather than waiting until it feels necessary.

02Sessions & Topics Covered

  • Career-path storiesHeard speakers describe their own career paths as anything but straight lines (moving between industries, even being let go before rebuilding), framed as proof that an unconventional path is not a failure.
  • Cross-functional framingNoticed how nearly every department introduction made a point of explaining how that team depended on strong relationships with other teams to do its job well, tying directly back to the business track’s point about cross-department handoffs.

03Reflection

The people met early in a career (including the people met during an internship) are often the same people who will refer, hire, or open a door years later, so the effort put into genuinely knowing them now is rarely wasted.

  • Relationship Building
  • Networking Early
  • Cross-Functional Awareness
  • Long-Term Reciprocity
