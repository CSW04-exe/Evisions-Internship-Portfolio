Projects/Department Weeks/Week 1
Week 1 · Product & UX

Product Management, UX, and the Software Development Life Cycle

01Summary

Sat with the Product and UX teams, who represent the customer inside Evisions and are responsible for identifying who the buyers are, what problems they have, and what is actually worth building.

Learned the internship’s throughline project: the Social Engagement Accelerator (SEA), a dashboard that aggregates student social data to flag isolated or at-risk students so staff can intervene early.

Got an overview of Evisions itself: 25+ years exclusively in higher ed, 900+ institutions served, and flagship tools like Argos, Argos X, and Data Agent.

02What I Worked On

  • Market segmentationCompared two contrasting institutions (a residential four-year university and a commuter community college) to see how differently “engagement” needs to be measured and created at each.
  • Grooming meetingSat in on a live grooming meeting, scoping real product tickets (a feature-flag removal, a delete-confirmation pattern, and AI feedback buttons) from request to hand-off.
  • RoadmappingBuilt a roadmap under a hard September 1 launch constraint, deciding which of twelve candidate features made the MVP and which were parked.
  • WireframingSketched a low-fidelity wireframe for the SEA dashboard, applying Gestalt principles and Nielsen Norman heuristics to the layout.

04Reflection

The week’s biggest lesson: get the market opportunity wrong and no amount of good engineering saves the product. Everything downstream depends on solving the right problem for the right segment. It reframed how I look at every feature request now, not as “can we build it” but “who is this actually for.”
